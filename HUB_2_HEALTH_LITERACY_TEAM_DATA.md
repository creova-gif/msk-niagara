## Hub Focus Areas

### Health Literacy Promotion
- **Language-accessible health information** (multilingual materials)
- **Community health education** workshops and programs
- **Health navigation support** for newcomers and refugees
- **Culturally responsive health services** development

### Target Communities
- **Immigrant populations** in Niagara Region
- **Refugee communities** (recent arrivals)
- **African descendant communities** (intersectionality with Hub #3)
- **Women and girls** (Future Black Female partnership)
- **Children and families** (Children's Services collaboration)

### Key Research Areas
1. Health information access barriers
2. Cultural competency in healthcare
3. Community-based health promotion
4. Multilingual health literacy resources
5. Social determinants of health for newcomers

## Hub Research Methodology

### Community-Based Participatory Research (CBPR)
- **Community co-leadership** (Mariam Khayinza, TOES Niagara)
- **Academic-community partnership** (Dr. Crawford + community partners)
- **Participatory action research** methods
- **Community members as research assistants** (e.g., Cecilia Nambalirwa)

### Multilingual Approach
- Research conducted in multiple languages
- Translation services integrated
- Culturally adapted research materials
- Community interpreters engaged

### Knowledge Mobilization
- Community workshops and training
- Plain-language health resources
- Public library partnerships for dissemination
- Health system integration

## Academic-Community Partnership Model

This hub exemplifies the MSK Partnership's commitment to **co-leadership** and **power-sharing**:

### Academic Leadership
- **Dr. Joanne Crawford** brings research expertise, institutional resources, and academic rigor

### Community Leadership
- **Mariam Khayinza** brings lived experience, community connections, and practical knowledge

### Shared Decision-Making
- **Consensus-based** research priorities
- **Community-driven** project design
- **Equitable resource** allocation
- **Collaborative knowledge** mobilization

This model ensures research is **community-relevant, culturally appropriate, and action-oriented**.
